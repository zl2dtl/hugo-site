About

Hi. I’m Dan.#

I made this site as somewhere to put things. Project notes, half-baked ideas, stuff that broke, stuff that worked, the occasional 2am spiral of “why is this not working” that somehow resolves itself by morning. No grand plan. Just a place that’s mine.

What’s here#

Imagine if my workbench had a baby with my journal and that baby got into the junk drawer. Sometimes you’ll find something genuinely useful — how I fixed a thing that shouldn’t have broken, why your Arduino sketch keeps crashing, a 3D model that actually prints without the supports staging a revolt. Other times it’s me at stupid o’clock, three coffees deep, chasing down some problem that’s been living rent-free in my head for days.

There’ll be step-by-step guides when I remember to document what I’m doing. Half-finished projects I got excited about and then quietly abandoned. Rants about why nothing works the way it’s supposed to. Probably too many photos of things that look broken but somehow aren’t.

No posting schedule, because that’s how you kill the fun. No clean categories, because that’s not how my brain works.

Who I am#

I’m ZL2DTL — licensed amateur radio operator, which explains the URL and the weird radio content you’ll stumble into. I spend a lot of time messing with microcontrollers, getting into arguments with OpenSCAD (before inevitably crawling back to Fusion on Windows), poking at circuits that were working fine until I decided to “improve” them, and building things that already exist — just not quite the way I want them to.

When I’m not doing any of that, I’m probably drinking coffee or listening to music. Sometimes both. Often both. The coffee is non-negotiable and the music taste is not up for debate.

I like taking things apart to see how they work, then putting them back together with extra parts left over. I enjoy solving problems that probably aren’t worth solving. I have strong opinions about documentation (never enough) and comments in code (also never enough — unless it’s my own code, in which case my previous points are null and void).

Oh, and I hate writing software. I will cheerfully spend a week on hardware to avoid writing fifty lines of code. Even this website, which was largely done for me by the marvellous developers of Hugo and the PaperMod theme, was hard enough.

Why this exists#

Partly because writing things down helps me figure out what I actually think. And when you spend hours wrestling with something stubborn, you might as well document it so future-you doesn’t suffer the same way. Every now and then someone messages to say a post helped them fix something, which makes the whole thing feel worthwhile.

But honestly? This is for me. It’s how I keep track of what I’ve learned, what I’ve broken, and what I’m working on next. The internet just happens to be where I keep my notes. Also, being able to casually drop a link to my own website in conversation will make me feel very important, and that counts for something.

If something here saves you time, or makes you feel less alone while wrestling with your own tech, then we’re both winning.
