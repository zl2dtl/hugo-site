Slowly, Then All at Once

March 13, 2026 · 4 min · 764 words · Daniel Liddle

She asked for a post and I had nothing. Which is a first, sort of. Usually there’s something rattling around worth writing about, but today it was just this vague sense that everything is moving both too slowly and too quickly at the same time, and I couldn’t work out how to make that into something worth reading. Then I realised that was exactly the thing worth writing about.

I remember when school felt like a drag. My earliest writing, the stuff from two or three laptops ago now, was full of it. It started on an old HP Elitebook, moved to the ThinkPad, then briefly lived on a Dell Latitude I picked up because it was small enough to actually carry to school without thinking about it. I gave the Latitude to my sister when I was done with it, which was the right thing to do, but the backups of everything I’d written went with it. She’s still using it, which is good. The ThinkPad is still sitting around despite being broken, which is less rational but I can’t quite bring myself to throw it out. Too much history in a thing that size.

So the writing is gone. A whole HP Elitebook worth of it, a ThinkPad worth of it, whatever made it onto the Latitude before I handed it over. All of it. What I do know is that I’ve become a better writer since then, which I can say with some confidence because I was quite bad at the start and I’m at least less bad now. The loss stings less knowing that. A bit.

And now school just sort of happens. I sit in English and accounting and physics and spend most of it thinking about circuit designs or nothing at all, and then it’s over and another week has gone by without much friction. It’s not that anything changed dramatically. I think I just stopped fighting it.

The strange part is that the period I remember as dragging the most, the part that felt like it would never end, now feels like it was years ago. Which it was, nearly. I started writing properly about a year ago and that already feels like a different chapter entirely. Time does that thing where it moves slowly enough to be painful in the moment and then in hindsight it compressed itself into almost nothing.

University is two years away. Two years sounds like a long time. It isn’t, I know that already, I just can’t feel it yet. But I’m aware enough to know that when I get there I’ll look back at right now the same way I look back at the early writing I no longer have. It’ll feel like it went by quickly. It’ll feel like it was both yesterday and a very long time ago simultaneously.

I wish I still had those early posts. Not because they were good, they almost certainly weren’t, but because they’d be a record of what it actually felt like to be in the slow part. The feeling is still there in memory but memory compresses things, edits them, makes them softer than they were. Writing doesn’t do that. Right now I’m in what will probably look like the fast part in hindsight, and I’m trying to pay attention to it. That’s partly what this is for.

She’s been there through all of it, before the writing even started. There’s a story I tell sometimes about my first real 3D printer project, the one that taught me more than I bargained for, and the retelling of it shifts depending on who’s listening. Not because the story changes, it doesn’t, but because different details come to the surface depending on what the person in front of me cares about. Tell it to someone technical and it becomes entirely about the pain and misery of writing printer firmware from scratch, and she might not come up at all. Tell it another way and she’s quietly at the centre of it, though I keep that version vague because I don’t particularly want to field questions about it. I speak vaguely about her impact on my life not because it’s small but because it’s the opposite, and some things don’t survive being explained to people who weren’t there.

The point is she’s been a constant through a period that hasn’t felt constant at all. Everything else has moved and shifted and some of it has been lost entirely, three laptops worth of it, but that part hasn’t changed. That’s not nothing.

With love, Daniel
