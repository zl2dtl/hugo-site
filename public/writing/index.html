Two Hours That Weren't Nearly Enough

We found what I called a very niche spot in the botanic gardens today, and time forgot how to move properly. He leaned into me first, or maybe I leaned into him, it’s hard to say where one gesture ended and another began. Suddenly we were cuddled on a bench, the world shrinking to the size of us. It was perfect in a way that stays with you. The kind of thing that makes you understand what people mean when they talk about missing someone who hasn’t left. He’s still mine and I already want more of this, more of how easy it felt, how right. More of the conversations that didn’t need forcing, the silences that didn’t need filling. ...
