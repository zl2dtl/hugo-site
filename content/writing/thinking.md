This one is late. Very late. But it's worth writing down before it gets any further away.

Last Sunday I went out with her. Coffee shop, then wandering around the shops, then back to the coffee shop because apparently once wasn't enough and I had absolutely no complaints about that. It was a good day. A genuinely, simply good day, which feels worth saying out loud because those aren't always as common as they should be.

The thing that stuck with me most wasn't anything that was said or anything that happened in particular. It was just that for a few hours my brain was quiet. Anyone who knows me or has read anything on this site will know that's not a small thing. The machinery that usually runs constantly, the problem solving, the overthinking, the running commentary on everything, just stopped. I wasn't thinking about what needed doing or what I should have said or what comes next. I was just there, in a coffee shop, with her, and that was the whole of it.

There's a specific moment I keep coming back to. Just sitting there, I was holding her phone and she was scrolling through reels, and I was just present for it. That's all. Nothing remarkable about it on paper. But there's something about being around someone you love unconditionally that just quiets everything down. No performance, no effort, just easy. I don't have a better word for it than that and I've been trying to find one.

Being present is something I have to actively work at. I'm not naturally good at it. My brain likes to be somewhere else, usually several places at once, usually at least one of them being the future. But last Sunday it didn't. She has a way of making the present feel like enough, which is not something I say about many things.

And yet the future only feels closer every day, and she's in it. I don't know the particulars, whether I end up in medicine or engineering or something else entirely, but I know that. She's the constant everything else gets built around. The details will sort themselves out.

The way I care about her isn't something I can easily compare to how most people I know care about their person. It's not possessive, it's not conditional, it doesn't come with strings. It's more that I would genuinely hate to see her fail, which I also know won't happen because she is the most dedicated person I know. But she worries, and I wish she wouldn't, and I care about that too. About all of it. About her being okay, and being happy, and getting everything she's working toward. In essence, I just care. Deeply, simply, without much complication beyond that.

With love, Daniel
