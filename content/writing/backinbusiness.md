Right. So. It's been a while.

A few months of radio silence across basically everything. The blog, the socials, all of it. Life got in the way in the way it sometimes does and somewhere along the line I just stopped. Not intentionally, it just sort of happened. But I'm back, and I had a pretty packed weekend, so let's get into it.

This weekend has been a funny mix of making money and spending it. About a year ago I started making 3D printed morse keys as a little side hustle. Nothing serious, just a way to scrape a bit of cash from the hobby. They were the first thing I ever sold, ever, and there's something strange and good about making actual money from something you built with your hands. I kept at it for a while then drifted off, as I tend to do. Then out of nowhere this weekend three orders landed at once. Not life changing stuff, about $50 all up, but that's not really the point. It got me back in the mood for it and that's worth something on its own.

The timing also helped, because I'd just had to drop $300 I really didn't want to spend on a new laptop after the ThinkPad met its end on a physics classroom floor. I was saving for a Vespa. A rubbish $1000 one, sure, but a Vespa nonetheless. The keys coming back at exactly this moment felt like the universe doing me a small favour, and I'll take it.

While all that was going on I built a microphone for a QMX, knocked out a 3D printed colour coded cable organiser as a little design job, and took on a Kenwood TS-50 recap. The TS-50 came down from a guy in Christchurch who sent it to me specifically, which is always a nice bit of trust to have placed in you. These radios are old enough now that the capacitors are well past their best, so I pulled it apart and replaced the lot. It went back better than it arrived, which is true, but I'll be honest, it's still not quite right. There's something in there I haven't fully chased down yet. Better than it was, not as good as it should be. We'll see if it comes back.

And then there's the Ender 6, sitting in pieces at Zero Waste right now. Zero Waste is the shop I volunteer at where we take in donated tech and restore it instead of letting it go to landfill. The plan with the Ender 6 is to get it running properly and use it at repair cafes, printing whatever parts are needed to keep things out of the bin. It's a CoreXY machine, theoretically faster and more capable than your average printer once it's dialled in. I say theoretically because right now it very much is not dialled in, and I'm looking forward to every frustrating minute of getting it there.

Working on it reminded me of fixing the da Vinci 1.0A, getting on for four years ago now. That machine had no business being as much fun as it was. It was a terrible printer and I loved it completely. The Ender 6 has that same energy. But the da Vinci memory doesn't just stop at the printer. It was also around that time that I met the first person who I just knew wasn't going anywhere. People come and go, that's just the nature of things, but every now and then someone comes along and something in your brain quietly goes oh. This one's different. This one's staying.

Which got me thinking about love as a concept. Not the greeting card version, the actual thing. I said to someone the other day that love and hate are equal magnitude vectors pointing in opposite directions. You can't hate something that intensely without caring about it just as deeply. The intensity is the same, just the direction changes, which means in some strange way they're the same feeling wearing different clothes.

I'm not great with words when it comes to this stuff. I feel things clearly enough, I just don't always manage to say them out loud. An I hate you from me carries a lot more weight than it probably should, because it usually means the exact opposite, and I think the people who matter understand that. I think it. I imply it constantly. I just haven't always been able to say it plainly. I'm working on that, slowly closing the gap between what I feel and what I actually articulate. This blog is part of that, if I'm honest. Writing things down and putting them somewhere outside my own head.

Oh, the joys of thinking like me. Can't even look at a printer without ending up here. And yes, quite literally, when I say I hate you, I quite literally mean the opposite. I would very much like to just say it. But it would also be a bit unnerving if british boy suddenly gained the ability to process emotions. These posts wander all over the place, from capacitors to philosophy to whatever this is, but they all end up in the same place, written for the same person. This one's no different.

With love, Daniel
