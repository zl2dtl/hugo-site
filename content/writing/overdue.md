So much for posting every month. So much for "I'll be updating this more." Here we are anyway.

It has been barely over a week since the dawn of my 16th revolution of the sun, which feels like a reasonable excuse to finally sit down and write something. A lot has happened in a short amount of time, mostly involving computers going wrong and computers going right, which is on brand.

I'll start with the bad news. The gaming laptop, the ROG, made a noise it shouldn't have made. Plugged it in to charge, heard a whine, and that was that. Won't turn on. Back it goes, almost inevitably, just to be at my loss in some way or another because that's how these things tend to work out. At this rate I'm starting to think the only reliable laptop is a ten year old ThinkPad, and the only thing that kills those is a physics classroom floor. I would know.

The good news is I've replaced it with something considerably better for the money. I picked up a used desktop and I'm writing this on it now. i5-9600K, 32GB of DDR4 at 3200, an RTX 3070, and about 1.5TB of storage spread across an NVMe and two SATA SSDs. All of that for $650, which I maintain is the best $650 I have ever spent. It lives in a massive Cooler Master ATX case, which earned it the very apt nickname of "the big computer." Relatively tasteful lighting, usually sitting on a solid blue or dark purple. It's a good machine. I'm happy with it.

And then there was the birthday. Sixteen years old, one week ago, and the best gift I received was a water bottle. I say that without a single trace of irony. She got me it, and it stores carbonated drinks, I use it every single day, and I appreciate practical things more than almost anything else. At risk of sounding like an unpaid advertisement, it is genuinely one of the best things I own. She knows me well enough to know that a useful gift lands better than anything else, and she was right, which is also on brand for her. A useful gift from the right person is worth more than anything showy, which I suppose is consistent with everything else I've ever written on this site.

More soon. Probably. I mean it this time.

With love, Daniel
